Transportation Security Administration Oversight

2019-10-29T20:01:13-04:00https://images.c-span.org/Files/594/20191029100754010_hd.jpgThe House Homeland Security Subcommittee on Transportation and Maritime Security held a hearing on the progress made in implementing the TSA Modernization Act, a year after it was enacted as a division of the FAA Reauthorization Act of 2018. Testifying were Patricia Cogswell, acting deputy Transportation Security Administration administrator, and William Russell, director of Homeland Security and Justice in the Government Accountability Office. Some of the areas discussed at the hearing were employee pay increases and retention, transportation security enhancements, the implementation of Real ID and its impact, streamlining TSA Pre-Check, and efforts to promote small business vendor partnerships, especially when looking at new security technologies.
