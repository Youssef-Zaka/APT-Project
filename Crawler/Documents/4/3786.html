What are Visual Abstracts?

Visual Abstracts are eye-catching, graphical summaries of your research. Because they combine image and text, they are easy to read and very effective at engaging your audience, especially on social media channels.

Think of a Visual Abstract as the business card for your research!

We turn your publication abstract into a cutting-edge Visual Abstract: Draw attention, communicate findings in seconds, and leave a lasting impact.


Scope
Currently, our Visual Abstract service is limited to medical research and to life science research. Only abstracts written in English language can be processed at this stage. We are working hard to expand our service to other scientific disciplines and other languages.

 

How can it help you?

  • Make an impact

Make a strong impact and increase your article views with Visual Abstracts optimized for social media channels.

  • Draw attention

Highlight your work with illustrations designed by a team with
decades of experience in science illustration.

  • Communicate well

Communicate your findings to a broad audience and in a fraction
of the time. Our Visual Abstracts are crafted to increase readability.

Problem - Information Overabundance

Does Information overabundance impact your work?

You are unable to find what you are looking for and even your best
work remains unnoticed.

There are more than 33 million citations on PubMed, with more than 1
million new ones every year: Visual Abstracts are designed to
increase your abstract’s visibility among the myriad of other
publications.


What makes our product best for you ?

  • Molded to human perception

Our Visual Abstracts were engineered to guide the reader's eye, increase reading speed and enhance content retention. The standardized Visual Abstract format will further increase your abstract's visibility.

  • Developed by and for researchers

Researchers from all over the world helped us to develop and to test our Visual Abstracts. In two separate studies Visual Abstracts increased reading speed by more than two-fold without reducing content memorization.
