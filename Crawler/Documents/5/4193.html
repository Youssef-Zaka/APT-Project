Dutch[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Unknown.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ʏk/
  • Audio(file)
  • Hyphenation: uk
  • Rhymes: -ʏk

Noun[edit]

uk m (plural ukken, diminutive ukkie n or ukje n)

  1. (Netherlands) small child, animal etc.

Kalasha[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

From Sanskrit उदक (udaka), from Proto-Indo-European *wódr̥.

Noun[edit]

uk

North Frisian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Old Frisian āk, from Proto-Germanic *auk. More at eke.

Adverb[edit]

uk

  1. (Föhr-Amrum) also; too

Plautdietsch[edit]

Zhuang[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Tai *ʔwuːkᴰ (“bone marrow”). Cognate with Bouyei ugt (“brain”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Standard Zhuang, Shuangqiao) IPA(key): /ʔuk˥/
  • Tone numbers: uk7
  • Hyphenation: uk

Noun[edit]

uk (Sawndip forms 腛 or 𰍷 or 喔, old orthography uk)

  1. brain (organ)
  2. brains (mental ability)
  3. mind; memory
