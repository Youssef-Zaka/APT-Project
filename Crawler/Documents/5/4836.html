Tom Fish in 2012

Heya! I am Guerillero, or Tom Fish. I am an administrator, functionary, and Arbitration Committee Clerk here on the English Wikipedia.

I joined Wikipedia in November 2009. In 2011, I was elected to be a director of the Featured Sounds process; I was a director until the process was marked as historical that November. Later that year, I passed my RfA and became an administrator. In 2012, I was appointed as an Arbitration Committee Clerk. I resigned from clerking a year later when I accepted a community position on the Audit Subcommittee. In 2014, I was appointed as an Oversighter. That December, I was elected to a two-year term on the Arbitration Committee and appointed as an interim Arbitration Committee Clerk due to a shakeup in the clerk office. When I left the Arbitration Committee in 2016, I retained my Oversight and CheckUser rights. I was rather burned out from being on the committee and was de facto semi-retired from 2016 to 2019. In 2018, I lost my Oversight rights due to inactivity, but was reappointed as an Arbitration Committee Clerk.

When I returned to active editing in 2019, I dove into content creation. I wrote audited content and a smattering of Did You Know? articles. I also got involved with doing source reviews for Featured Article and Featured List candidates. As you can see from the list below, my interests are varied. I have written about folk music, punk rock, fine art, Washington College, religious brothers and sisters of the Catholic Church, and other weird things. I have a list of my ongoing projects on my talk page. I am currently making maps for articles. If you have something that you would like to see, please ping me.

In my real life, I am a trailing spouse, cartographer, and recovering emo kid. I currently reside in Copenhagen, Denmark with my wife and our rescued Black Lab-Great Pyrenees mix. I write FishEye, a newsletter about geography, technology, politics, and culture and how they intersect.
