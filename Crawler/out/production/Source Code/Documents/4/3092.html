Garbage Disposals That Make Kitchen Clean-Up Easy

Lulu Chang

Contributor

One of your most useful kitchen appliances is one that you rarely see, but what it lacks in aesthetics, it makes up for in function. We’re talking about your garbage disposal, the indispensable tool that grinds up food and flushes them away. Garbage disposals are an efficient and effective solution for common food waste.

“There are two types of garbage disposals: batch feed and continuous feed,” explains Jessica Petrino, educator and appliance expert at AJ Madison. “The difference is how the disposal gets turned on. Batch feed disposals require a piece to be inserted and twisted inside the sink. You must activate it intentionally and, for this reason, it is considered the most family-safe option.”

Continuous feed disposals are more popular, though, Petrino notes. “A light switch or countertop button activates continuous feed disposals. Once activated, they churn continuously until turned off.” 

When it comes to finding the right disposal for you, Petrino recommends considering the power of the appliance to match your needs. “Garbage disposals are measured in horsepower,” she says. “Most models range from 1/3 HP to 1 and 1/4 HP. The lower power models are recommended for small kitchens and tight spaces; higher power models take up more space under the sink but are more effective at grinding up food particles.” 

To help you narrow down your search, we’ve rounded up some of the best continuous feed garbage disposals we’ve found across the Internet.
