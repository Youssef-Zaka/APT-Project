Gender Pay Gap Report 2021

Gender Pay Gap Report 2021

IOP Publishing (IOPP) has always been committed to the principle of equal opportunity and equal treatment for all employees and this is an area in which our employees score us highly via our engagement surveys. Our focus on equality and diversity is woven into the fabric of who we are and expressed as both an expectation and a commitment in our Code of Conduct, key policies and processes, Continuous Improvement programme and employee selection and education.

We know that the success of our work relies on our ability to recruit, nurture and retain a good mix of talented employees and we ensure employees are paid equally for the same or equivalent work, regardless of gender, or any other characteristic. All roles are measured through an objective job evaluation process and salaries are benchmarked using recognised third-party sources.

The table below shows our mean and median gender pay gap as at the snapshot date of 5 April 2021. It also captures the mean and median difference between total bonuses paid to men and women across IOPP in the year up to 5 April 2021, i.e. for the 2020/21 performance year.

Mean Median
Pay gap 12.45% 18.29%
Bonus gap 12% 14%

 

We are pleased to report an improvement in our mean pay gap over the 2020 report, acknowledging however that the median pay gap widened. We continue to be aware of the challenges that affect the pay gap, including the types of roles that men and women sit within the organisation and the salaries that these roles attract. This has resulted in a lower number of female employees in the top-quartile position during 2020/21.

With all of that said, we have held our course in ensuring that our policy for recruiting the best person for the job still stands, but we have layered in to our work with managers a focus on reducing bias in the recruitment and promotion process. We plan to make further strides in this area in 2022.

We award an annual performance-related bonus to the majority of our employees. The eligibility criteria apply equally across the board and the bonus gap itself can largely be attributed to the number of males and females in part time roles, although we are pleased that the gap itself has significantly reduced in this reporting period.

Proportion of employees who received a bonus

Male 93%
Female 89%

 

In the 2020-2021 period we awarded an annual bonus to all eligible employees who achieved or exceeded a good standard of performance.

Pay quartiles

The table below illustrates the gender distribution across IOPP over four equally sized quartiles.

Proportion of employees by quartile male/female

Lower quartile 3o% 70%
Lower middle quartile 46% 54%
Upper middle quartile 46% 54%
Top quartile 63% 37%

 

Our gender pay gap is not a subject about which we are complacent, and we are committed to continuing our drive to promote diversity and equal opportunity in our workforce.
