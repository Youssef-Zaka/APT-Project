† insameikle, adv.

falsefalse

$Revision$
Forms:  late Middle English insomekylle (northern); Scottish pre-1700 insameikl, pre-1700 insameikle, pre-1700 insamekle. (Show Less)
Origin: Formed within English, by compounding. Etymons: in adv., so adv. and conj., mickle pron.
Etymology: < in adv. + so adv. and conj. (compare β. forms at that entry) + mickle pron. (compare δ. forms at that entry).
 
Compare insomuch adv.(Show Less)
Scottish and north dialect. Obsolete.

  = insomuch adv.

1483   Cath. Angl. 196/2   In so mekylle, adeo, eatenus.
1562   N. Winȝet Wks. (1888) I. 42   Insamekle that the sewin diacones..wes presentit afore the Apostlis.
1588   A. King tr. P. Canisius Cathechisme or Schort Instr. 137   Insameikl that S. Peter..said, Ȝe haue slane the authoure of lyf.
