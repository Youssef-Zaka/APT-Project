Origin: A borrowing from French. Etymon: French code.
Etymology: < Anglo-Norman and Middle French, French code collection of laws or statutes (early 13th cent. in Old French), collection of data (c1339), book, volume (1486), collection of rules and regulations (1752 or earlier), official list of approved drugs (1835 or earlier), system of conveying information with signals and symbols (1866), set of conventions of a human language used to communicate meaning (1916) < classical Latin cōdic-  , cōdex   (see codex n.).

 a. Originally: (a collection of regulations setting out) a system of military, naval, or maritime signals (as a contextual use of sense 2a).  b.
