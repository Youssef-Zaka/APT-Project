soaker, n.

falsefalse

Brit. Hear pronunciation/ˈsəʊkə/
, 
U.S. Hear pronunciation/ˈsoʊkər/
Forms:  Also 1500s–1700s soker (1500s sooker ?).(Show Less)
Frequency (in current use):  Show frequency band information
Etymology: < soak v. + -er suffix1.(Show Less)
Thesaurus »
 

†1. A drainer, exhauster. Obsolete.

1577   B. Googe tr. C. Heresbach Foure Bks. Husbandry i. f. 40   It is a great soker of the grounde.
1610   W. Folkingham Feudigraphia i. x. 31   Wheate, Barley, Woade,..are great impairers and soakers of the soyle.
1630   P. Massinger Picture sig. G4   I found By a sad experience, there is no such soker As a yonger spongie wife.
a1634   J. Day Parl. Bees (Lansd. 725) f. 24   Hees a male Powle catt, a mere hart blood soaker.

1577—a1634(Hide quotations)

 
Thesaurus »
 

 2. An immoderate drinker; a drunkard. Cf. 3b.

1593   Bacchus Bountie in Harl. Misc. (1809) II. 265   The greatest soakers shal be least controulde.
1652   Charac. Low Countries 60   The Dutchman would still be the perfectest soker.
1679   A. Wood Life & Times (1892) II. 460   The black pot men carried it for Perot, a thorough paced soaker.
1771   Ann. Reg. 1770 ii. 240   'Tis Soakers like me..That enable you Brewers to ride in your Coaches.
1840   R. H. Barham Look at Clock in Ingoldsby Legends 1st Ser. 54   Amusing himself..With a couple more soakers, Thoroughbred smokers.
1897   T. C. Allbutt et al. Syst. Med. II. 865   In the case of a soaker on the verge of delirium tremens.
figurative.
1593   T. Nashe Strange Newes in Wks. (Grosart) II. 242   A scholler in nothing but the scum of schollership, a stale soker at Tullies Offices.
1665   R. Brathwait Comment Two Tales Chaucer (1901) 52   I know you for a notable Soaker; you cannot endure a Sharer.
1700   W. Congreve Way of World iv. i. 63   The Sun's..an honest Soaker, he has a Cellar at your Antipodes.

1593—1897(Hide quotations)

 
 3. old soaker:
Thesaurus »
 

 a. An old hand at anything; an old stager.Perhaps originally with allusion to drinking (cf. 3b, 2), but this does not appear in the quotations.

1590   R. Harvey Plaine Percevall sig. C   An olde sooker, that caries such Pottical verses of the State of Flanders, in a linnen bag.
1593   G. Harvey Pierces Supererogation 194   He was an old soaker indeede: and had more witt in his hoary head, then six hundred of these floorishing greene heads.
1614   G. Meriton Christian Mans Assuring House 35   A young sinner is easily converted; but olde soakers are hardly reclaimed.
c1670   H. Newcome Diary (1885) 142   These old soakers with their Record's Arithmetic.

1590—c1670(Hide quotations)

 
Thesaurus »
 

 b. An old hand at drinking; a regular toper.

1665   S. Pepys Diary 15 Feb. (1972) VI. 36   A very good dinner among the old Sokers.
1670   J. Eachard Grounds Contempt of Clergy 31   A task..that would much better fit some old soker at Parnassus, than his sipping unexperienc'd Bibbership.
1699   B. E. New Dict. Canting Crew   An old Soker, a true Pitcher-man.
1829   W. Scott Anne of Geierstein II. vii. 218   Some quiet old soakers, who were already beginning to think of the reckoning.
1863   Mortons of Bardom I. 177   Thus assailed, the old soaker was compelled to withdraw.

1665—1863(Hide quotations)

 
 4.
 

 a. One who soaks something.

1611   R. Cotgrave Dict. French & Eng. Tongues   Trempeur, a dipper; wetter, moistener; soaker, steeper.

1611—1611(Hide quotations)

 
Thesaurus »
Categories »
 

 b. A drenching rain.

1789   J. Byng Diary 29 May in Torrington Diaries (1938) IV. 95   An approaching Storm made me pull up near the Grey Hound Inn; and well I did, for it came down a Soaker.
1839   T. Hood Ode to St. Swithin v   Mother of all the Family of Rainers! Saint of the Soakers!
1857   A. Mayhew Paved with Gold (1858) ii. v. 112   The rain came down in streams of water... ‘Here's a soaker!’ thought the young Bohemian.

1789—1857(Hide quotations)

 
Thesaurus »
 

 c. A soaking pit.

1928   Jrnl. Iron & Steel Inst. 117 201   The heated soakers are fired by blast-furnace gas.
1959   Jrnl. Iron & Steel Inst. 193 368/2   The soaking pit used was an electric soaker with a single trough coke resistor.
1976   Steel USSR VI. 194/2   The holding period from the end of casting to charging into the soakers was maintained constant as far as possible.

1928—1976(Hide quotations)

 
Thesaurus »
Categories »
 

 5. A sheet of lead used in roofing to keep out heavy rains.

1895   Jrnl. Royal Inst. Brit. Archit. Mar. 351   Hips should have hip-tiles and not soakers.

1895—1895(Hide quotations)

 

Draft additions September 2020

Categories »
 

  Chiefly Canadian. The action or fact of getting one's foot or feet wet, especially as a result of stepping into a puddle. Frequently in to get a soaker .

1987   Toronto Star 9 Jan. (Final ed.) a3/1   What good is a ducky in this country if you can't wade through slush at an intersection without getting a soaker?
1997   Calgary (Alberta) Herald (Nexis) 8 June (Final ed.) d11   The water was cold and my bare foot kept sinking into the muddy silt... But what's a soaker when there's wild watercress to be picked?
2020   @gMudry 17 Mar. in twitter.com (accessed 25 Mar. 2020)    When I stepped off the curb I got a soaker when I sunk my foot in a puddle, gutted.

1987—2020(Hide quotations)
