Origin: Apparently formed within English, by compounding. Etymons: an element of unknown origin, -phobia comb. form.
Etymology: < a first element of unknown origin + -phobia comb. form.
It has been suggested that the first element derives from Byzantine Greek κωλοβαθριστής   stilt-walker ( < Hellenistic Greek κωλόβαθρον   stilt < ancient Greek κῶλον   limb (see colon n.2) + βάθρον   base, pedestal: see anabathrum n.), but this does not satisfactorily account for the form, and the element may be of arbitrary origin.

  Extreme or irrational fear of clowns.

1997   alt.tasteless.jokes 30 May (Usenet newsgroup, accessed 3 Dec. 2019)    ‘34 Reasons Why You Should Hate Clowns’... They have big feet... They have a phobia named after them. (coulrophobia, more commonly known as clownophobia).
2010   Jung Jrnl. Winter 60   With their masked faces, strange clothing, and unpredictable behavior, clowns have instilled fear in many. In fact, the term coulrophobia is defined as a persistent, abnormal, and irrational fear of clowns.
2013   @mselibrary 16 Sept. in twitter.com (accessed 1 Oct. 2019)    Coulrophobia affects a lot of people (especially those who saw IT and Poltergeist).
