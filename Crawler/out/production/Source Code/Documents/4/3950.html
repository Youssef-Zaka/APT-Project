Etymology: Initialism < the initial letters of polymerase chain reaction n. at polymerase n. Compounds.(Show Less)
Biochemistry and Molecular Biology.
Categories »
 

  A technique for producing multiple copies of a chosen sequence of DNA (or in later use also RNA) in a sample, using repeated cycles of denaturation, annealing with primer, and replication with DNA polymerase; = polymerase chain reaction n. at polymerase n. Compounds. Also as a count noun: an instance of this; a test employing this.When PCR is used to produce copies of an initial RNA sequence, an additional primary step is involved in which the enzyme reverse transcriptase is used to transcribe the RNA into DNA.PCR increases the amount of a specific sequence of DNA present in a sample, allowing it to be detected and identified.Recorded earliest as a modifier; cf. Compounds 1.

1985   R. K. Saiki et al. in Science 20 Dec. 1350/3   The β-globin segment was amplified by the polymerase chain reaction (PCR) procedure of Mullis and Faloona.
1993   Latest Word Dec. 27/1   PCR is an extraordinarily sensitive technique and currently the fastest and most certain method of detection of the HIV virus available.
2005   Proc. National Acad. Sci. U.S.A 102 19188/1   PCRs were run in triplicate for each brain sample, and at least three independent sample pairs were used for each statistical analysis.
2014   Compar. Clin. Pathol. 23 669/1   Blood samples from 100 Esfahan dogs were analysed by PCR to evaluate haemoplasma infection status.
