English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin gratis.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK) IPA(key): /ˈɡɹɑː.tɪs/
  • Audio (RP)(file)
  • (US) IPA(key): /ˈɡɹætɪs/
  • Rhymes: -ɑːtɪs

Adjective[edit]

gratis (not comparable)

  1. Free, without charge.
    • 2010 September 27, Ann Charters; Samuel Charters, quoting John Clellon Holmes, Brother-Souls: John Clellon Holmes, Jack Kerouac, and the Beat Generation‎[1], →ISBN, page 162:
      Really syncophant[sic] stuff, but it may help. It's gratis in any case.
    Synonym: (used in the free software movement to distinguish from libre, "free as in speech") free as in beer

Translations[edit]

free of charge — see free of charge

Adverb[edit]

gratis (not comparable)

  1. Free, without charge.
    • c. 1596–1599, William Shakespeare, “The Second Part of Henry the Fourth, […]”, in Mr. William Shakespeares Comedies, Histories, & Tragedies […] (First Folio), London: […] Isaac Iaggard, and Ed[ward] Blount, published 1623, OCLC 606515358, [Act IV, scene iii]:
      I know not how they sold themselves: but thou, like a kind fellow, gavest thyself away gratis; and I thank thee for thee.

Related terms[edit]

  • gratuity
  • gratuitous

Translations[edit]

free, without charge — see for free

See also[edit]

  • libre

Anagrams[edit]

  • striga, trigas
