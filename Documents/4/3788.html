What you need to know

  • MSU will continue its COVID-19 vaccine/booster mandate for the 2022-23 academic year.
  • Masks are no longer required in most indoor settings, except for classrooms, research spaces, health care facilities and veterinary medical facilities. Effective May 16, MSU will drop its mask mandate for classrooms and research spaces as well.
  • The Early Detection Program and PCR testing at the Clinical Center will both end May 13 (those with medical/religious exemptions will no longer need to test as part of their exemption). Other testing options can be found here. At-home tests also are available to order for free via https://www.covidtests.gov/. 
  • A second booster dose also is available for those age 50 and older. Though recommended by the CDC, it is not required to fulfill MSU's vaccine/booster mandate.

COVID-19 testing

On-campus PCR testing is being offered both at the MSU Clinical Center and free of charge to members of the MSU community via the Early Detection Program. Off-campus testing also is available at several East Lansing-area locations.
