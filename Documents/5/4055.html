The Best Dry Shampoo for Every Hair Type

Kaleigh Fasanella

Contributor

Here’s a little-known secret about clean hair: You don’t need to wash your hair every day. In fact, a daily wash can dry out your scalp and hair, which can lead to dandruff and broken ends. That’s where dry shampoo comes in.

Dry shampoo is usually made of cornstarch or rice starch and typically comes in an aerosol can, but it is also available as a mist and in loose powder form. It absorbs oil from the scalp to degrease your hair and eliminate odors. Dry shampoos can also add volume and texture.

“I like to use it as a styling tool to create lift at the roots,” says New York City-based colorist and salon owner, Nikki Ferrara. “It’s easy, fast, and effective.”

How to Use Dry Shampoo

Even though dry shampoo can help you increase the time between washes, it isn’t a replacement for the real thing. Felicia Leatherwood, celebrity natural hair guru and founder of Brush with the Best, recommends washing two to three days after you’ve used a dry shampoo to give your scalp a good cleansing and refresh your mane. “You definitely don’t want to use it too often because it will clog your pores, and that will make it difficult for your hair to grow,” Leatherwood says. It’s best to only use the oil absorber on super greasy days, or when you’re in a pinch and don’t have time to wash your hair before an occasion. With that said, for those who are often on-the-go or who work out daily, dry shampoo can be a largely well-tolerated lifesaver.

Before You Buy

When it comes to shopping for dry shampoo, like most things it really comes down to personal preference. As you’ll learn below, common ingredients include powders, clays, and starches, and you’ll find they come in an array of different formats, scents, and shades. Hence why it’s important to do your research before buying, so you can ensure you score the best one for your specific hair type, color, and needs.

All this to say: Here are some excellent, pro-recommended dry shampoos to consider:
