thatrightthere , 04/29/2022

Tom Cruise on Oprah’s Couch

Honestly this podcast title popped up on my iPhone screensaver. I’ve listened to maybe one podcast on my phone. I even had to re-find the podcast button. The title caught my eye because I remember when the iconic “couch jump” happened. I did not see it live but heard about it and saw the still photos like everyone else. I loved how the podcasters pulled out the relevant details and historical perspective of that time. Cruise, a superstar, walks onto an Oprah set full of a 100% female audience screaming and practically fainting over him. I loved how they delved into his personality and how it encountered what seemed like a welcoming “safe space”. With his type A, “maverick”, always positive attitude - it really didn’t surprise me that he’d do the “Top Gun” fist pump (hilarious!); or clench Oprah’s hands in some Zen-like power grip (still hilarious). But what struck me is how the authors pointed out that most of us NEVER saw the event live. Like most of us did not have dvr’s then; & that YouTube just began in 2005; & the Oprah “you get a car…” was less than a year prior.
So it was interesting since most of us NEVER saw the actual show; only still photos of Cruise on the couch; and we only heard the the narrative that he was acting crazy over his new girlfriend who was half his age -
our brains filled in the rest by imaging this superstar A list actor jumping up and down on the couch like a monkey; like some horny old man with a new girlfriend half his age.
That could not have been FURTHER from the truth. So Cruise really got a bad rap back then. But, looking back at all the hype, the couch, the show, the women in the audience, going crazy, Oprah doing what Oprah did best - you gotta admit, it’s still pretty hilarious!
