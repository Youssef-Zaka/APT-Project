Biographical details[edit]

I used to have my full name on here, but people started getting this as the top hit when they googled my name. You can easily find it in the article history if you're interested. I currently live in the UK and am 37 years old (using the {{age}} template).

I have a PhD in astrochemistry and work as part of the editorial staff of Science, one of the world's top scientific journals. I completed my PhD at the University of Nottingham, where I was subsequently a postdoctoral researcher. After that I worked at the Royal Astronomical Society, on their journal Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society (MNRAS). I have also held visiting positions at the Southern African Large Telescope and European Southern Observatory. As a professional astronomer, I conducted research into the interstellar medium, primarily using observational optical and infrared astronomy; I continue to conduct astronomical research in my spare time. These days I work in scientific publishing as a journal editor. I therefore declare a potential conflict of interest on all those topics.

Hobbies and interests[edit]

I have a professional interest in physics and astronomy, and have always been interested in history, particularly English history from the Heptarchy onwards, and European history between classical antiquity and the Napoleonic wars. I'm also interested in politics, and am a keen reader of newspapers and other news sources, particularly The Guardian.

Wikipedia interests[edit]

I've been editing Wikipedia since summer 2005, when I got annoyed by finding minor mistakes in my favourite online reference and just had to fix them. I signed up for an account in November 2005. To begin with, I mostly just fixed mistakes in pages I was looking at anyway, and didn't go out of my way to add anything. Over the years I've become more involved in the running of the place, particularly the Main Page, and done some content creation.

I can mostly be found hanging around Talk:Main Page, WP:ERRORS, WP:In the news/Candidates and their associated pages. Related to this, I sometimes contribute to writing or improving articles which appear on the In the News section. I'm also a member of WikiProject Astronomy, often contributing to areas raised at WT:AST and WT:ASTRO. I tend to have a slow burning 'project' article, usually on a topic in astronomy, though I rarely have enough real-life time to move these along very quickly. I have rollback and reviewer user rights, though I don't tend to use either of them very often.

Licensing[edit]

In general, I am averse to anyone using my work for commercial gain without permission. I'm not a fan of Wikipedia's licenses allowing this, although if I want to contribute here I don't have much choice. However, I am a great fan of releasing content to others to use, so long as they don't make money from my time and effort. Similarly, I don't like anyone claiming someone else's work as their own or without proper attribution, and try not to do so myself.

In order to allow others to make use of content they created, and to reduce the problems created with multiple licences, I hereby release any edit I mark as 'minor' into the public domain.
