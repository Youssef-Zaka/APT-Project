figshare is a repository where users can make all of their research outputs available in a citable, shareable and discoverable manner

we offer

Figshare for Institutions

A home for papers, FAIR data and non-traditional research outputs that is easy to use and ready now

Figshare for Publishers

Integrate with the researchers’ workflow for a more complete coverage of data and materials, and for a seamless process when transferring data to get reviewed, curated and published

Figshare+

Share the datasets and materials supporting a specific publication or project with a one-time Data Publishing Charge (DPC), storing data securely and redundantly in the cloud for a minimum of 10 years

Figshare for Biotech and Pharmaceutical Organizations

Manage and showcase plain language summaries (PLS), supplemental data, and other digital research content

our path so far

  • We launched Figshare Alpha as a platform for researchers to make their data a first-class research output.
  • 2012
  • Figshare V1 officially launched on January 17, 2012 with API capabilities for programmatic uploading and customization. Figshare for Publishers is launched with Portal and journal-integrated Viewer functionality. Launch partner and integration with ORCID.
  • 2013
  • We introduced a freemium model to allow anyone to share their research data openly and implemented a citation button and GitHub integration to get a DOI for software.
  • 2014
  • Figshare for Institutions launched as an out-of-the-box repository infrastructure with a suite of functionality including branded DOIs, enhanced metrics including Altmetric, user management, and HR feed and SSO integrations.
  • 2015
  • We made improvements to our enterprise solutions including DOI versioning, enhanced functionality for public profile pages, descriptions on group landing pages, private links, public statistic pages, and mobile-friendly public pages.
  • 2016
  • We launched projects for collaboration and collections for grouping data together, implemented OAI-PMH and API V2 including updated documentation, advanced search, Google Dataset markup, account impersonations and an unpublish tool for administrators, and batch editing.
  • 2017
  • We added citation counts to item pages sourced from Dimensions, an item review module, dynamic citation styles, custom licenses, ResourceSync, RSS feeds, and advanced custom metadata.
  • 2018
  • Virus scanning and iThenticate were implemented for Figshare for Publishers. To make Figshare for Institutions suitable for all content types, not just data, we implemented custom domains, a funding metadata field, a ReadCube integration for displaying Open Access paper PDFs, GDPR compliance features, and follow/e-alerts across all enterprise offerings.
  • 2019
  • Significant development work took place to bring the public pages of Figshare in accordance with various accessibility standards including EN 301 549, WCAG 2.1 AA, and Section 508. We implemented support for Handles and made improvements to our item review module.
  • 2020
  • We developed restricted publishing functionality to allow researchers to share their outputs with users within their Figshare group, with users in an IP range, or just with administrators.
  • 2021
  • We introduced integrations with GitLab and Bitbucket, as well as improvements to our existing ORCID integration and a Make Data Count integration. We launched an integration with Sherpa Romeo to allow users to check the open access policies of their publishers.
