Watch coral create conveyor belts of mucus to share food

Reef-building coral may feed more efficiently by using tiny hair-like structures – called cilia – to generate food-carrying conveyer belts of mucus and seawater across their surface. By adding a marker to the surfaces of several coral species, Igor Adameyko at the Medical University of Vienna and his colleagues were able to model their flow.
