Airlander 10

Largest flying aircraft

This is currently the largest aircraft flying that uses innovative technology to combine characteristics of fixed wing aircraft and helicopters with lighter-than-air technological advances to create a new breed of hyper-efficient aircraft. It can stay airborne for up to five days at a time if it is manned, and for over 2 weeks unmanned. It will complete a wide range of communication, cargo carrying and survey in both the military and commercial sectors all with a significantly lower than normal carbon footprint than other forms of air transport.

Airlander 10 is underpinned by the company’s numerous patents that is worldwide. From the latest materials in technology, to the aerodynamic effects of its shape, it is full of innovation. There is no internal structure that is in the Airlander – it maintains its shape by the pressure stabilisation of the helium inside the Airlander, and the smart and strong Vectran material it is made of. Carbon composites are used all throughout the aircraft for strength and weight savings.

The latest news is Hybrid Air Vehicles Ltd can confirm a mooring line attached to the Airlander did contact a power line outside the airfield.  No damage was caused to the aircraft and this did not contribute to the heavy landing.  We are sorry for any inconvenience this may have caused to anyone.
