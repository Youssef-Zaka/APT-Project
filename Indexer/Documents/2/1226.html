Best Broadband Deals in the UK

The best broadband deal for you will depend on what you need from your service. If you just use the internet for social media and emails, then a cheap package with low average speeds could be good for you. If you binge-watch Netflix shows and do a bit of online gaming, you should go for deals offeringfast download speeds.

If youre often on the move, the best option may be either a no-contract internet package or a mobile broadband deal. Look atmobile dealsfrom providers like Vodafone and EE to find the best mobile broadband deals. Ultimately, the best broadband deal is one that offers the services you need for a monthly price you can afford. Were here to help you compare broadband providers and their deals to ensure you get one that meets your needs.

Picking the Best Broadband Deal for You

Choosing the best internet plan can be easier when you can see and compare all of the packages you could get side by side.

Knowing the important factors upfront like;

  • Your internet usage
  • Your budget
  • The broadband speed youre after
  • Additional services or features you want
  • A contract length that works for you
  • The provider with the best customer service
  • Any added perks, extras, freebies or vouchers you can get by switching
  • Which deals are available in your area
  • New routers, hardware, or professional installation and whether a setup cost or activation fee will be charged

Dont be fooled by providers who claim to have the best deal. Your needs will be unique to your internet usage requirements. While there is no one-size-fits-all internet package thats best for everyone, weve compiled a few factors that can help you compare and make an informed decision based on whats most important to you.

Usage

Internet usage is often the main factor in determining which broadband deal is best for you. How often do you use the internet and what are you using it for? If you find yourself barely having time to check your email and dont tend to stream TV, the best deal for you will be very different from someone who works from home, binges series, or frequently plays games online.

Affordability

What is your budget and does that budget align with your usage? If youre a high usage customer, youll want at least a mid-tier package, but if you choose the cheapest plan, youll likely end up frustrated when your service cannot deliver the activities you care about. Its better to understand what speed you need first and then compare deals in that range to find one that balances your budget and lifestyle.

We recommend paying attention to the standard pricing offered by providers as a guide. Users are often reeled in by a special offer and are caught out when the offer ends and theyre put on the providers standard pricing.

Speed

The general rule is: the higher the average speed, the higher the average monthly cost. Although its generally believed that its best to have the highest speed possible, its useless to pay extra for a speed you dont need. If your internet usage only involves regular browsing and there arent many users or devices in your home, broadband that offers 30Mbps will be fine for you and help you save compared to a high-speed plan. This underlines the importance of step 1: its extremely important to understand your usage.

Availability

It is very important to check whether the broadband deal you want is available in your area. If you live in a remote area, you might not be able to enjoy a fibre-optic broadband connection. Similarly, some broadband providers might have betterbroadband coverage in your areathan another one. Its a good idea to check which providers are available near you early on in the process of shopping for a broadband deal, so you dont waste time comparing plans that wont be available to you.

Additional services

Are you also looking for additional services likephone and call allowancesor features likeTV bundles and entertainment deals? Instead of finding a separate deal for each of these services, you can find a bundle deal that offers all these services together. Bundling these services always results in a lower price than purchasing them separately, so it is important to know what you want before choosing your broadband deal.

Hardware requirements

This is often overlooked when shopping for broadband service, but knowing your hardware requirements can save you money in the long run. One provider may have the cheapest deal, but if that deal comes with a hefty setup cost or requires you to purchase new equipment, you may end up spending more than you expected.

If youre switching from BT to Sky, you will need aSky broadbandrouter at your home. Equally, if youre switching to Virgin Media for the first time, youll need new wiring installed to link up to its cable network. If the price of that equipment pushes you over the budget you have set for your broadband, you will likely be better off finding a deal that does not require hardware upgrades.

Length of contract

The benefit of a longer contract is that the payment is divided into smaller chunks. On the flip side, longer contracts mean that you are tied to a particular deal for a longer period, and if you want to switch internet providers, youll have to wait longer or end the contract by paying contract exit fees, which is often just as expensive as staying with your existing provider.

If you move frequently, you could find that the best deal for you is a month-to-month broadband service, which works similarly to many pay-as-you-go mobile deals. Though the average monthly cost may be more, youll save by not having to buy out your contract when you move. However, if you have a home that you dont intend to move from for several years, you can save quite a bit by choosing a lengthier contract.

No matter what you choose, its important to be aware of your contract end date. Most promotional pricing only lasts until the end of your contract period - after that date, the offer ends and your broadband provider is free to increase prices as they see fit.

Customer service

No matter how good your internet provider is, you will always have to talk to customer service at some point. Whether its your initial setup, paying a bill, or dealing with an issue, its comforting to know that youll be dealing with a friendly and efficient customer service team.Talktalk,Vodafone, andVirgin Mediareputedly have some of the best customer service teams among broadband companies.

Added perks and extras

Some broadband providers offer freebies to promote their deals. Prepaid cards, cash back, vouchers are relatively common free perks often included in new customer deals. Gadgets, such as laptops, tablets, or mobile phones, are much less common but not unheard-of. This definitely something to keep an eye out for.

Free routers are common, being included in most packages, and most providers include line rental in the price of their internet. If you dont want a phone line, some providers do offer broadband without line rental for less - Virgin Media is a notable example.

You will want to compare deals that offer free gifts against similar deals that dont offer them. Often, the cost of the gift is simply added to the monthly price of your broadband.If the equivalent package is cheaper, then consider whether you really need a the gift at all.

Picking the Right Broadband Speed

The average speed of your internet is measured in megabits per second (Mbps). A download speed of 30Mb per second will allow you to download 30Mb of data in one second. To put it in perspective: a 5-minute YouTube video playing at 1080p quality is around 170Mb, so a download speed of 30 Mbps will get through it in under 6 seconds.

The download speed of your broadband package affects streaming quality while watching shows and movies, and can result in buffering if your download speed isnt fast enough. Although you always want to have internet service fast enough to keep up with your online activity, its not always worth paying the highest price for the fastest broadband speed. Its important to match the speed you pay for with your actual usage.

Upload speed is another factor that some users will want to consider. Not often advertised by providers, a packages average upload speed is the speed at which you can upload data to the internet. This will be particularly important to you if you have a YouTube channel or a Soundcloud account which you regularly add content to, or if you often have to send large files at work. Bear in mind that deals with slow average download speeds will have even slower average upload speeds.

An average speed of 30 Mbps may suit a user who only uses the internet for regular browsing or social media. However, if you stream a lot of videos, are into online gaming, or have a large household with many connected devices, you may need something faster.

0-35 Mbps

Best deal for:Checking email, web browsing, social media
Devices: 1-2

This speed is suitable for a home with one or two users online at a given time. It will work for those who dont do much with the internet except for web browsing and social media. Netflix claims you need 25 Mbps to stream 4K content and only 5 Mbps for streaming HD. However, if you plan on having anything else connected to your broadband at the same time, youll need to opt for a higher speed. Ideally, this speed is for light internet usage.

45-100 Mbps

Best deal for:4K streaming and online gaming
Devices: 3-5

This speed range is generally for households having 3 to 5 connected devices at any time with moderate to high internet usage. If youre looking to stream 4K video, this will be an ideal range. For fans of online gaming, this speed range should keep you running smoothly without interruptions.

100-920 Mbps

Best deal for:Working from home, downloading large files, live video streaming Devices: 6+

100 Mbps to 920 Mbps represent the fastest broadband speeds available in the UK currently and provide the best speeds you can get. For those who want the best connections and the fastest download speeds, these are the deals to compare. These speeds can handle the heaviest workloads and are perfect for people who work from home and frequently share large files.

While some could save money by selecting an internet plan with lower average speeds, a 2020 study showed that, on average, UK citizens have access to9 connected devicesin their home. Ranging from tablets to headphones and game consoles to speakers, the average person is using their broadband connection on much more than just their laptop or smartphone.
