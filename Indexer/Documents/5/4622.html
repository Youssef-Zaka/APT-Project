#MyFlickrYear - Let's talk about yours!

cabb88 says:

Hello, Flickr members!

This year marks the second launch of #MyFlickrYear. We introduced a number of improvements based on your feedback to ensure that more of you that participated on Flickr throughout 2021 received the report.

If you haven't yet, look through the #MyFlickrYear2021 tag to see some of your fellow community member's Flickr achievements. If you received #MyFlickrYear2021, let's talk about it!

- Was the "most popular" photo in your #MyFlickrYear2021 your own personal favorite?

- Did your stats around faveing and commenting encourage you to set any new goals when it comes to showing appreciation for and having conversations around photography?

- Shoutout your best friends on Flickr! What do you love about their photography?

- Last but not least, are there achievements that you would like to see highlighted in next year's #MyFlickrYear? What are they and why are these achievements important to you and your photography?

Thank you for being part of the Flickr community, for helping each other achieve your photography goals, and for sharing and celebrating your accomplishments with #MyFlickrYear! We hope that you're enjoying this annual initiative.

Carol and the Flickr Team

vaneramos says:

Even as I was taking my "most popular photo" of 2021 I knew it was something special. I was only taking photos in my garden in the rain, and you can't even tell it's raining from the photo, but the quality of light was supernal. I would like to give more comments... but considering that many I received were only copy and paste, and I try to avoid doing that, I guess I'm satisfied with the amount of commenting I did. I would rather give comments when it comes naturally, and when I mean it, so I won't be setting any goal to give more this year.

I'm a numbers person so I enjoyed seeing all the stats in the MyFlickrYear email, especially since it was my first year on Flickr so I was sort of finding my way throughout the year. It was interesting to see the faves and comments received/given. I don't participate in any groups that require commenting and I always try to give personal comments and write something specific to the image I am commenting on, so I was a little surprised to see the total number of comments I had written (higher than I was expecting!).

My most popular photo, and also highest ranked in Explore (#62) which likely had partly to do with the high number of faves it received, was also my personal favorite. I knew when I was taking it that the moment was a special one, the combination of the fog and the light stopped me immediately as I was hiking and I knew I had to try capture a photo of it.
