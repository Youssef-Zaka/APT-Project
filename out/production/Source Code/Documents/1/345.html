Search Tip

A wildcard is a special character you can use to replace one or more characters in a word. There are two types of wildcard. The first is a question mark ?, which matches a single character. The second is an asterisk *, which matches zero or more characters. The two kinds of wildcard can be mixed in a single search.

Search Tips & Abbreviations

Search results for 'algorithm':

algorithm noun 1 any procedure involving a series of steps that is used to find the solution to a specific problem, eg to solve a mathematical equation. 2 computing the sequence of operations, often represented visually by means of a flow chart, that are to be performed by and form the basis of a computer program. algorithmic adj.
ETYMOLOGY: 1930s in sense 1: from earlier algorism arithmetic, from Latin algorismus, named after Al-Khwarizmi, a 9c Arab mathematician.
