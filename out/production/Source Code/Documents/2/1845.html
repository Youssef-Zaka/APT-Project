What is MIT World?

MIT World is a free, open streaming media web site of the most significant public events at MIT. It features the most recent speakers and guests from across the campus and around the world. It is a project of the Professional Education Programs at MIT's School of Engineering.

| TOP |

Information for MIT Event Hosts
 
How is material selected for MIT World?

Video content selected for MIT World must be first and foremost of broad interest to the community. It should be scholarly and relevant, and at the same time accessible to a wide audience. Areas of content can include the latest information in science, engineering, architecture, humanities, arts and sciences, technology and management, as well as specific seminars and presentations that are technical in nature, or illuminate general trends in society, the arts, and humanities. The site does not contain public relations, administrative or human resources communication, nor does it operate as a news site. It does however, link to multiple relevant home pages, source materials, the MIT News Office or other school, lab, department and administrative sites for background and more information.

| TOP |

What are the production considerations?

Video for MIT World is produced by MIT Video Productions, a division of AMPS. Contact MVP at 617 253-7603.

Videos can be shot on the following video formats:

Beta
DCam
Mini DV
SVHS
Hi 8

If an event host wants to have an event produced by someone other than MVP, production considerations should be discussed well in advance. MIT World reserves the right to turn down an event if it does not meet professional video production standards.

Event hosts are encouraged to provide logos and digital photographs of the event for inclusion in the web page that features the video.

| TOP |

What are the costs involved?

The host of the event bears the cost of video production. All video production details and costs should be discussed in advance with MIT Video Productions. Once the event has been taped, MIT World typically covers the cost of digitizing and creating the Real Player streams, permanent hosting on MIT World's video server, web page development and production. Event hosts may have other uses for the videotape, such as providing dubs for constituents and/or using the material for other web or non-web purposes. MIT World does not require exclusive access to the video material, however permission for any other use of the material needs to be addressed between the speaker(s) and the event host.

| TOP |

How do I get my event on MIT World?

Contact MIT World Project Manager Laurie Everett as soon as you know the date and speaker for your event. (leverett@mit.edu) 617 253-1350. If the content meets MIT World editorial guidelines, and video production costs are covered, the speakers for the event will need to sign an appearance release. This release gives MIT World permission to put the lecture on the web.

| TOP |

How can I get the appearance release?

The MIT World appearance release is enclosed here in a PDf File or is available from the project manager.

| TOP |

How long after the event is it available on MIT World?

Events are posted on MIT World anywhere from 2-8 weeks following the event. While the production considerations require an approximate 2 week turn around, there are peak periods of event production that require finished lectures to be in a queue for posting on the web. MIT World is updated with 2 new videos every 7-10 days. The busiest months for events at MIT are October, November, February and April.

| TOP |

What happens once the video is available on MIT World?

On the day that a new video is posted, an email goes out to MIT World's ever-growing mailing list. Event hosts are encouraged to forward that email on, or rewrite it for various constituencies such as staff, faculty, students, alumni, and funders.

Once a lecture is on MIT World, the host is also strongly encouraged to link to it from the host's home page. The link should be directly to the information page about the event, not the MIT World home page, as the home page will change often. MIT World is able to work with hosts on cross promotion, spotlights and other ways to encourage a broad audience to view an event. Finally, MIT World is able to provide traffic information to the host including web statistics and number of times video has been viewed.

General Information
 
How many videos are on MIT World?

MIT World adds approximately 75 new videos per year. They come from all 5 Schools at MIT, and a wide range of labs, programs, centers and departments. All videos are permanently archived in the MIT World database, and are available on demand 24/7.

| TOP |

How do I view the videos?

To view MIT World videos you need an Internet browser such as Internet Explorer or Netscape versions 4 or higher with Java script enabled. You also need the free media player, RealPlayer to be installed on your computer. (For more information go to MIT World's Help page.)

| TOP |

How many connection speeds are offered?

Each video on MIT World is available in 3-5 of the following bit rates:

32k audio only
56k modem
80k broadband
150k broadband
220k broadband

| TOP |

Why does MIT World use RealPlayer and not other media players?

MIT World does not provide video streams in other video formats, such as Windows Media Player or Apple Quick Time. While these options are technically possible, there are significant costs involved in providing multiple formats, as well as storage concerns on the video server. Given the wide range of users for MIT World, RealPlayer is the best option for the project at this stage of development.

| TOP |

Are transcripts, audiotapes or videotapes available from MIT World?

No. Transcripts, audiotapes and videotapes of events are not available from MIT World, however such materials may be available from the original host of the event. A link to the host of the event is always included on the web page, and should be contacted directly.

| TOP |

Can I download videos onto my computer?

No. Videos can be streamed on-demand to a computer, but cannot be downloaded, edited or duplicated.

| TOP |

How did MIT World get started?

Founding organization, Center for Advanced Educational Services (CAES)
Professor Richard C. Larson, Director
Professor of Civil and Environmental Engineering
Professor of Engineering Systems

MIT World was launched in September 2001 at the Center for Advanced Educational Services, and was created by Professor Richard C. Larson, Director. The service was designed to use video streaming technology to enable students, faculty, staff and alumni to have access to the events that happen every day on the MIT campus. The idea took off after years of development and testing. Many former staff of CAES played significant roles in the research, development and launch of MIT World. CAES ceased operations in June 2003. MIT World is now part of the School of Engineering's Professional Education Programs.
