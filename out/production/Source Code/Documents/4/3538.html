Origin: Of uncertain origin. Perhaps a borrowing from a language of Sierra Leone.
Etymology: Origin uncertain; perhaps < a language of Sierra Leone, perhaps either (a) with remodelling by association with coquet n. and -oon suffix, or perhaps (b) (if taken from a Creole language) ultimately reflecting an otherwise unattested derivative of French coquet  coquet adj.
J. E. Gray, who first used this name (compare quot. 1846) cites it alongside a location in Waterloo on the West Coast of Sierra Leone.

  A small antelope of western and central Africa, Cephalophus rufilatus, which has a reddish-brown body with a pale belly and dark grey back and legs. In later use also coquetoon antelope.Now called red-flanked duiker.Now only in lists of alternative names for the animal.

1846   J. E. Gray in Ann. & Mag. Nat. Hist. 18 166   The Coquetoon, Cephalophus rufilatus.
1864   J. G. Wood in Boy's Own Vol. Christmas No. 225   The Coquetoon is a trifle redder than the Bush Goat, and has the chin and abdomen pale yellow.
1954   A. P. Gray Mammalian Hybrids 73   Cephalophus rufilatus J. E. Gray [Coquetoon Antelope, Little Red Antelope. Red-flanked Antelope].
