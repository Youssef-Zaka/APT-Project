Origin: Formed within English, by compounding. Etymons: trans adj., masculine adj.
Etymology: < trans adj. + masculine adj.
Compare the following earlier instance, apparently in the sense ‘representing the experience of all men; representative of a common rather than individual male identity’ (hence < trans- prefix   + masculine adj.):
1990   R. L. Perelman Illegible Narr. Modernist Genius (Ph.D. diss., Univ. of Calif.) i. 38   Bloom the transmasculine counter in Joyce's mythic schemes has to touch all the water on earth when he fills the kettle.
(Show Less)
 

  Designating a person whose birth sex was female but whose gender identity is aligned with or characterized in some way by masculinity; of or relating to such a person.Transmasculine is typically used as an umbrella term to refer to a wide variety of people and their identities, including but not limited to trans men and non-binary individuals.

1999   Village Voice (N.Y.) 7 Dec. 6/1   Transgender people—and especially those who are masculine-identified—are courageously beginning to mobilize and speak out.., as was proven by..New York City's first-ever conference specifically addressing transmasculine communities.
2014   H. Simmons & ‘F. White’ in L. Erickson-Schroth Trans Bodies, Trans Selves i. 11   The terms transmasculine, often used by trans men, and transfeminine, often used by trans women, can convey that we fall generally to one side of a gender binary, but that we are not limited by the binary.
2021   @taylora3_ 3 June in twitter.com (accessed 11 June 2021)    I can be nonbinary and transmasculine and present however I want because gender is bloody complicated.
