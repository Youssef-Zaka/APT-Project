 1. (The name of) the fifteenth letter (Ο, ο) of the Greek alphabet, corresponding to the short o. Also figurative.In transliterating ancient Greek, usually rendered as o (in ancient Greek used to distinguish a short from a long o sound: cf. omega n. 2).

1631   S. Jerome Arraignem. Whole Creature xiii. 208   The whole Globe..cannot fill this little triangulary heart: so many Omicrons, cannot fill one little Delta.
1659   T. Pecke Upon Certain Woman in Parnassi Puerperium i. 30   With wondrous speed, in stature, large you grow: Omicron the last year; Omega now.
1727   A. Pope Occasional Poems in Miscellanea I. 77   Omicron and Omega from us Wou'd each hope to be O in Thomas.
1728   E. Chambers Cycl. at O   The Greeks had two O's, viz. Omicron, ο, and Omega ω.
1762   Philos. Trans. 1761 (Royal Soc.) 52 32   The last letter here would have been equivalent to the Greek Omicron.
1853   B. St. John Village Life in Egypt 147   When I was learning Greek and had got as far as my omicron, I should have been ashamed to contradict Moses.
1893   E. M. Thompson Handbk. Greek & Lat. Palaeogr. x. 135   The very small size of theta and omikron, may also be noticed.
1903   E. Fawcett Portraits in Voices & Visions i. 9   If, quoting Greek, you once presumed to write An omicron in place of an omega.
1947   Jrnl. Investigative Dermatol. 9 213   Many such Greek verb stems..end in o represented by omicron when the pronunciation is short (ŏ) or by omega (ω) when it is long (ō).
1999   D. F. Wallace Brief Interviews with Hideous Men 309   He came to her with..some kind of ritualistic design or glyph like a..palsied omicron drawn on his tenebrous brow.

 2. Astronomy. Used in the name of the fifteenth star of a constellation. Frequently written ο.The use of Greek letters to designate the stars in a constellation was introduced by J. Bayer in his Uranometria (1603). There is an approximate correspondence between the ordinal positions of the stars in this series and their brightness.

1796   W. Herschel in Philos. Trans. (Royal Soc.) 86 195 (table)    Lustre of the stars in Aquarius... ο 5.
1880   Proc. Amer. Philos. Soc. 1878–80 18 239   A very large meteor..passed directly over Omicron Ursæ Majoris, and disappeared just below Eta in the same constellation.
1899   R. H. Allen Star Names 131   ο1, a red star of the 4th magnitude, and π, a double,..with other small stars in the body of the Dog, were the Chinese Ya Ke, the Wild Cock.
1915   Astrophysical Jrnl. 41 255   A nebulosity close south of Omicron Persei is mixed up with a number of small stars.
1992   Astron. Now July 36/1   They include Mira itself (omicron Ceti) which can be seen shortly before dawn.

1796—1992(Hide quotations)
