Pronunciation: 
Brit. Hear pronunciation/ˈhaljuː/
, 
U.S. Hear pronunciation/ˈhɑlˌju/
Forms:  2000s– hallyu, 2000s– hanllyu, 2000s– hanlyu, 2000s– hanryu. Also with capital initial.(Show Less)
Origin: A borrowing from Korean. Etymon: Korean hallyu.
Etymology: < Korean hallyu (also transliterated as hanlyu) < Han-  , combining form of a name of Korea (see hanbok n.) + lyu   ( < Middle Chinese; compare Chinese liú   stream, current, category: see ryu n.), probably after either Japanese hanryū   (beginning of the 21st cent., or slightly earlier) or its etymon Chinese hánliú   (1998, apparently earliest in Taiwan).
 
Compare Korean wave n. at Korean adj. and n. Compounds.(Show Less)
 

  The increase in international interest in South Korea and its popular culture, esp. as represented by the global success of South Korean music, film, television, fashion, and food. Also: South Korean popular culture and entertainment itself. Frequently as a modifier, as in hallyu craze, hallyu fan, hallyu star, etc. Cf. Korean wave n., K- comb. form .The rise in popularity of South Korean culture, which began in the 1990s in East and South-east Asia, had become a worldwide phenomenon by the 2010s, driven largely by the success of various forms of Korean entertainment on social media, video-sharing platforms, etc.It is unclear whether quot. 2001   reflects the Korean word, or its equivalents in Chinese or Japanese (see etymology).

[2001   Variety 27 Aug. 29/3   The ‘Korea Current,’ or ‘hanryu’ as it is called locally, started in 1998, when Korean rock group Clon hit Taiwan. Korea fever spread to mainland China and Hong Kong, carried through TV, movies, games, food and fashion.]
2003   Korea Herald 8 July 10/1   Kim and a host of other Korean pop stars..have become an increasing force in the Chinese popular music market, riding on the ‘Hanryu’ or ‘Korean wave’ phenomenon... Many of the problems for ‘Hanryu’ stars reside in the impossibility of determining the exact extent of their popularity.
2005   Korea Times 12 Jan. 6/1   The craze for South Korean pop culture, or ‘Hallyu’, in China, Taiwan, Vietnam and Cambodia is receding much earlier than expected.
2012   Guardian (Nexis) 29 Dec. (Travel section) 2   In 2011 alone, hallyu is said to have contributed more than £2.3bn to the South Korean economy.
2015   @sherinseba 30 Nov. in twitter.com (accessed 26 Apr. 2021)    I love Hallyu and Kpop is my obsession.
2020   Korea Times (Nexis) 8 July   We will continue to work to create attractive video content so some 100 million hallyu fans around the world can experience Korea through K-dramas and movies.
