Origin: Formed within English, by derivation. Etymons: anarchy n., anarchism n., anarchist n., anarchist adj., -o- connective.
Etymology: < anarch- (in anarchy n., anarchism n., anarchist n., anarchist adj., etc.) + -o- connective.
Compare French anarcho- (formations in which are attested from the late 19th cent., e.g. anarcho-socialiste (1893 or earlier)), and German anarcho-, Russian anarxo- (formations in both of which are attested from the early 20th cent.).
(Show Less)
 

 1. Forming nouns and adjectives with the sense ‘both anarchist and ——’ or ‘having anarchist values or characteristics’. Cf. anarcha- comb. form.See also anarcho-capitalism n., anarcho-syndicalism n.

1887   Milwaukee (Wisconsin) Daily Sentinel 14 Mar. 1/3   Upon entering Mueller's hall one was struck by..the almost total absence of the blood red hue so dear to the anarcho-communistic party.
1907   Times 23 Aug. 7/5   The Anarcho-Socialists may perhaps have agreed to differ peaceably.
1931   Amer. Mercury Jan. 53/1   The dour old anarcho-individualism was in our blood.
1969   Punch 17 Dec. 996   Anarcho-pacifists with strong lunatic leanings, their anti-imperialist non-violent protesting has, over the years, grown gradually more militant.
1977   ‘S. Leys’ Chinese Shadows (1978) vii. 148   The Workers-Soldiers Propaganda Teams of the Thought of Mao Tse-Tung were deployed in schools throughout China..to restore discipline and crush the anarcho-revolutionary movements.
1994   Computers & Humanities 28 53/2   The radical wing is represented by the anarcho-educational Internet, whose true believers want to remain as non-commercial as possible.
2014   Church Times 19 Sept. 15/2   There is a widespread sympathy for the old anarcho-nihilist sentiment: ‘Don't vote, because, whomever you vote for, the Government gets in.’

1887—2014(Hide quotations)

 
 2.

Categories »
 

  anarcho-punk   n. and adj.  (a) n. a style of punk rock music having anarchist lyrical content, or which otherwise promotes anarchism; (also) a performer or fan of this music;  (b) adj. of or relating to this music.In quot. 1976   not as a fixed collocation.

[1976   Sounds 30 Oct.   The emergence of the anarcho, punk (?) new (!) wave in the clubs of London which show signs of re-establishing the link between youth and integrity in rock.]
1979   Fifth Estate (Detroit) 18 Apr. 23/1   Anarcho-punk posters available for free from Ultra on request.
1986   Sunday Mail (Queensland) (Nexis) 30 Mar.   These alternative youth..harbor a variety of musical tastes from folk to punk, including obscure bands, imported music, anarcho-punk and jazz/rock.
1993   Guardian 31 Aug. ii. 6/1   The Levellers got where they are via the ardent support of pop's self-styled underclass—the New Age travellers, crusties and anarcho-punks who populate the free-festival and squat scenes.
2007   New Yorker 17 Dec. 42/1   I went really hardcore really quickly. Suddenly, I was into Killing Joke and Grass. A pair of British anarcho-punk bands.

1979—2007(Hide quotations)
