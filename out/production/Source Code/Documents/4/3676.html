follically, adv.

falsefalse

Pronunciation: 
Brit. Hear pronunciation/ˈfɒlᵻkli/
, 
U.S. Hear pronunciation/ˈfɑlək(ə)li/
Frequency (in current use):  Show frequency band information
Origin: Formed within English, by derivation. Etymons: follicle n., -ally suffix.
Etymology: Irregularly < follic-   (in follicle n.) + -ally suffix.
 
Compare follicly adv., and also earlier (more regularly formed) follicularly adv.(Show Less)
humorous.
Categories »
 

  With regard to the hair or hair follicles. Chiefly forming compounds, esp. in follically challenged: going bald, balding, bald (see challenged adj. b at challenge v. Additions); = follicularly challenged at follicularly adv. 2.follicularly adv.

1991   Times 17 July 10/3   The hirsute should, we are told, avoid referring to baldness as such and use instead the term ‘follically challenged’.
1993   Independent (Nexis) 13 Jan. 25   Jacques Vert..bids lamely to join the dictionary of politically correct expressions. After ‘vertically challenged’ and ‘follically disadvantaged’ comes JV's..: ‘Jacques Vert Plus—a collection for the fuller figured lady.’
1996   Q Jan. 120/5   Too short and follically underblessed to ever carve out a superstar niche for himself.
2003   Times 14 Mar. 55/2   Gregory is too slick, follically or otherwise.
2005   Press (Christchurch, N.Z.) (Nexis) 12 Mar. 13   Like the follically gifted Donald Trump before her, Martha is to host her own version of The Apprentice.
2012   B. Kannel Follow Me i. 9   You'll recognize that I'm follically challenged. Bald would be more direct.

1991—2012(Hide quotations)
