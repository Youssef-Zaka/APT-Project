Origin: A borrowing from early Scandinavian. Etymon: Norse busk-r.
Etymology: Middle English busk  , < Old Norse busk-r (Danish busk  , Swedish buske  ), cognate with Old High German bush   (Middle High German busch  , bosch  , German busch  ), Middle Dutch busc  , bosc   (Dutch bosch  , bos  ), all < Rom. bosco   or late Latin boscum  , boscus   wood, of which the ulterior source is unknown. Compare boscage n., bosk n.   The form busk is still found in northern dialect, but in Sc. is reduced to bus, buss; the buss of the Ayenbite was only Dan Michel's way of spelling bush with ss for sh.(Show Less)
