    Origin: Formed within English, by clipping or shortening. Etymon: English coronavirus disease 2019.
    Etymology: Shortened < coronavirus disease 2019 < coronavirus n.   + disease n.   + 2019, the year in which it was first identified.
    The β. forms   show a less common shortening of the same expression.

    Medicine.
    Thesaurus »
    Categories »
     

      An acute disease in humans caused by a coronavirus, which is characterized mainly by fever and cough and is capable of progressing to pneumonia, respiratory and renal failure, blood coagulation abnormalities, and death, esp. in the elderly and people with underlying health conditions. Also: the coronavirus that causes this disease.Covid-19 was originally identified in China in December 2019 and became pandemic in 2020.The virus that causes Covid-19 was provisionally called 2019 novel coronavirus (2019-nCov) and formally named severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) in March 2020.

    2020   Novel Coronavirus (2019-nCoV) (World Health Organization Situation Rep. 22) 11 Feb. in who.int (Internet Archive Wayback Machine 11 Feb. 2020)    Following WHO best practices for naming of new human infectious diseases,..WHO has named the disease COVID-19, short for ‘coronavirus disease 2019’.

    2020   Guardian 25 June 4/1   Fears remain over the risk of a second wave of Covid-19.
