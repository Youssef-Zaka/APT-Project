K-leg, n.

falsefalse

Brit. Hear pronunciation/ˈkeɪlɛɡ/
, 
U.S. Hear pronunciation/ˈkeɪˌlɛɡ/
, 
West African English Hear pronunciation/ˈkeˌlɛɡ/
Origin: Formed within English, by compounding. Etymons: K n., leg n.
Etymology: < K n. + leg n., so called on account of the resemblance of a sufferer's legs to the shape of the letter, especially if only one knee is turned inwards (compare quot. 1842).
With sense 1   compare the rare, but slightly earlier semantic parallel †X-leg and its probable model German X-Bein, usually in plural (both 1851 or earlier).

 1. In singular and plural. A condition in which one or both of a person's knees are turned inwards, resulting in a noticeable gap between the feet when standing with the knees touching; = knock-knee n.   Also (depreciative): a person affected with this condition. Now chiefly Nigerian English.

1842   Children's Employm. Comm.: 2nd Rep.: Trades & Manuf. App. Pt. II q28 in Parl. Papers 1843 XV. 1   There is most commonly only one leg turned in a—K leg [read —a K leg].
1889   A. Barrère & C. G. Leland Dict. Slang, Jargon & Cant I. 523/1   K legs (printers), a term of derision applied to a person with knocked-knees, or otherwise ‘shaky on the pins’, owing to the legs being apart as in the lower portion of a capital K.
1993   M. E. Ezekiel Windsongs II. 20   The first guy I ever took a shine to thought my legs were too crooked... The only respect and attention I got from him and his pals were sneering shouts of ‘K leg’.
2017   Nigerian Tribune (Nexis) 9 June   The Public Notice..stated that applicants with disabilities including those with..K-legs..are not eligible to apply.

1842—2017(Hide quotations)

 
Categories »
 

 2. Nigerian English. In singular and plural. A problem or flaw; a setback, an obstacle.

1981   C. N. C. Asomugha Nigerian Slangs 44   To have k-leg, used to describe the sudden collapse of a plan, proposal or deal.
2017   Sun (Nigeria) (Nexis) 8 Dec.   All the..projects that have developed K-legs in the state are directly or indirectly linked to him.

1981—2017(Hide quotations)
