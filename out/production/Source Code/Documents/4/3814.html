Expert Recommendations. Instant Access.

Description

Oxford Bibliographies is a sophisticated online recommendation service that provides you with original scholarly content used and trusted by professional researchers worldwide. Our thorough database provides a convenient and comprehensive introduction to the essential body of literature that has shaped scholarly thought. At the click of a mouse, you have 24/7 access to expert recommendations that have been rigorously peer-reviewed and vetted to ensure scholarly accuracy and objectivity.

The site is designed to save you time and increase your productivity. The user-friendly interface and quick links lead you directly to the information you're looking for--whether it's a chapter, book, journal article, Website, blog, or data set. Features such as saved searches, personalized citation lists, and seamless links to full-text print and online content allow you to quickly and easily create syllabi, compile course texts, and find supplemental materials. RSS alerts notify you when articles and bibliographies are updated, ensuring that you're using the most current knowledge in your field.

Exploring a topic that's new to you? Oxford Bibliographies helps you orient yourself within the literature and identify the leading authors, key works, major debates, and ideas that have shaped the scholarly conversation. An Editor in Chief, Editorial Board, and team of peer reviewers for each discipline ensure a balanced perspective, unparalleled accuracy, and up-to-date content.

Unlike old-fashioned bibliographies or indexing services, Oxford Bibliographies is a highly selective, peer-reviewed, and continually updated article service conceived and designed for researchers and students. Combining the deep knowledge of leading scholars with a contemporary online search tool, Oxford Bibliographies puts concise, reliable, and relevant information at your fingertips.

Coined the 'pre-Oldowan' or 'Lomekwian' after the site in West Turkana at which they were found, these tools are larger and cruder than the more recent Oldowan industry.
