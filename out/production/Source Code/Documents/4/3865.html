† ydraw, v.

falsefalse
View as:

  • Full entry

$Revision$
Forms:  see y- prefix   and draw v.(Show Less)
Origin: A word inherited from Germanic.
Etymology: Cognate with or formed similarly to Middle Dutch ghedrāghen   to carry, to contain, to bear (offspring, fruit), to endure, suffer, to reach (Dutch gedragen  , now chiefly (reflexive) ‘to behave’), Old Saxon gidragan   to carry, bring, to produce, to bear (offspring) (Middle Low German gedrāgen  , gedrēgen  , also ‘to wear, to endure, suffer’), Old High German gitragan   to carry, bring, to endure, suffer, to gather together, (reflexive) to behave (Middle High German getragen  , also ‘to wear’, German †getragen  , also ‘to bear (offspring)’), Gothic gadragan   to pile up, heap together < the Germanic base of y- prefix   + the Germanic base of draw v.
In Old English a strong verb of Class VI.
(Show Less)
Obsolete.
 

 1. transitive. To sweep (something) with the hem of a garment.

OE   Prudentius Glosses (Corpus Cambr. 223) in H. D. Meritt Old Eng. Glosses (1945) 28/2   [Ut tener incessus uestigia syrmate] uerrat : drage, gedræge.

OE—OE(Hide quotations)

 
 

 2. transitive. To endure, suffer (illness). Cf. draw v. 20.

OE (Northumbrian)   Lindisf. Gospels: Matt. ix. 20   Mulier quae sanguinis fluxum patiebatur duodecim annis : wif ðiu blodes flouing uel iorning geðolade uel gedrog tuelf uinter uel tuelf ger.

OE—OE(Hide quotations)

 
 

 3. intransitive. To cause a person to go from one state to another. Cf. draw v. 18.

lOE   Homily (Tiber. A.iii) in Englische Studien (1885) 8 473   Welan to fræcednisse gelaþiað, welan to forwyrde gedragað.

lOE—lOE(Hide quotations)

 4. transitive. To draw (a weapon). Cf. draw v. 54a.

lOE   St. Margaret (Corpus Cambr.) (1994) 168   Gedrah þu þin swurd..and þa fæmne þu ofsleah.
a1475  (▸?a1430)    J. Lydgate tr. G. Deguileville Pilgrimage Life Man (Vitell.) l. 3037   A cause evydent That thow mayst wel..The swerd ydrawe.

lOE—a1475(Hide quotations)

 5. transitive. With out. To subtract (a number). Cf. draw v. 26.

?c1425   Crafte Nombrynge in R. Steele Earliest Arithm. in Eng. (1922) 12   Þou schalt sett þat on ouer þe hier figures hed, of the quych þou woldist y-draw oute þe neyþer figure yf þou haddyst y-myȝt.

?c1425—?c1425(Hide quotations)
