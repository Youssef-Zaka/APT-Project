cowgirl, n.

falsefalse

Pronunciation: 
Brit. Hear pronunciation/ˈkaʊɡəːl/
, 
U.S. Hear pronunciation/ˈkaʊˌɡərl/
Origin: Formed within English, by compounding. Etymons: cow n.1, girl n.
Etymology: < cow n.1 + girl n., after cowboy n.(Show Less)
Thesaurus »
Categories »
 

 1. A girl or young woman who tends or herds cows. Now historical except in sense 2.

1753   tr. F. Génard School of Man 20   Hannah was the Cow-Girl of the Village.
1841   Lady Eastlake Resid. Shores Baltic I. xii. 141   The cow-girls and sheep-boys, &tc., all come in for their meals at stated times.
1884   E. Barker Through Auvergne 119   We passed a group of cow-girls singing.
1997   A. Lovelace & J. White Beliefs, Values & Trad. 16/2 (caption)    Krishna is often pictured in Vrindavan, where he lived among the cowherds and charmed the gopis (cowgirls).

1753—1997(Hide quotations)

 
Categories »
 

 2. spec. Esp. in the western United States: a woman or girl who herds cattle on an open range or (later) a ranch, usually working on horseback; a female cowboy, esp. as represented in films, novels, etc., of the genre of the Western (see western n.2 4).

1883   Daily Nevada State Jrnl. 6 Nov.   If she had not been captured, this young lady might have become the wild cowgirl of the Texas prairie, and afforded material for scores of dime novels.
1884   Boston (Mass.) Jrnl. 28 Nov. 2/3   A beautiful cowgirl lives near Murkel, Taylor county, Neb. She owns some stock, which she personally looks after.
1948   N.Y. Times 27 Oct. 29/6   A broncho-busting cowgirl, one of a rodeo troupe.
2014   Western Horseman Mar. 53/1   The event has grown to 30 four-person teams of cowboys and cowgirls that compete in events representative of their lifestyle.

1883—2014(Hide quotations)

 
Thesaurus »
 

 3. A position for sexual intercourse in which a woman sits astride a man, facing him. Cf. reverse cowgirl n.Sometimes also describing similar positions used by same-sex partners.

1993   R. J. Stoller & I. S. Levine Coming Attractions iii. x. 133   Cowgirl is where the girl's facing the guy and sitting up.
2006   Daily Record (Glasgow) (Nexis) 18 Sept. (Features section) 3   Doggy style is the most favoured sexual position for both sexes in our survey... A close second was the women on top or the cowgirl position.
2013   M. Lawson Deaths x. 318   Jonny had..cried himself dry for two weeks, until he met Vicky Bellows, a Historian who introduced him..to cowgirl, driving against him as she sat astride.

1993—2013(Hide quotations)

 

Compounds

 

 C1. General use as a modifier, as in cowgirl life, cowgirl work, etc.

1887   Bozeman (Montana Territory) Weekly Chron. 1 June (advt.)    ‘Cowgirl’ saddles, of many patterns.
1899   G. H. Dodge et al. What Women can Earn 305   I drifted into the ‘cowgirl’ life and dairy work almost without knowing it.
1949   Desert Sun (Palm Springs, Calif.) 28 Oct. ii. 1/6   Little Bear, sure sign of the season, came in last week from her cowgirl duties and plans to stay until the last doggie is looped.
2020   Sun (Nexis) 23 Mar.   Britney was most happy when participating in some sort of cowgirl work.

1887—2020(Hide quotations)

 
 C2.
 

 a. As a modifier, designating clothing or footwear regarded as characteristic of a cowgirl.Recorded earliest in cowgirl hat n.

1897   Lamar (Colorado) Reg. 25 Sept.   Cowgirl hats at Irwin's.
1912   N.Y. Times 15 Apr. vii. 9/2   My father gave up mining and bought a large ranch..and I began to wear ‘cowgirl’ clothes of blue flannel and buckskin.
1950   Chicago Defender 8 July 10/2   Guests partook of a ranch menu served by waitresses in cow-girl costumes.
1989   Quilting Today Dec. 59   We've also packed away several cowgirl outfits and fancy red boots, plus every dress she ever loved.
2019   Daily Tel. (Nexis) 8 Mar. 23   While some of the designs were dubbed ‘weird and unwearable’ by shoppers, there were a couple of standout pieces, including a brown suede A-line cowgirl skirt.

1897—2019(Hide quotations)

 
 b.
 

  cowgirl boot   n. a boot extending to mid-calf of a style associated with cowgirls, typically made of cowhide or synthetic leather, and having a pointed toe, moderately high, square heel, and decorative stitching or tooling; cf. cowboy boot n. at cowboy n. Compounds 4.

1925   Western Daily Press 23 Nov. (6:30 a.m. ed.) 5/7   The top boots the women are wearing to-day are certainly not Rodeo or Cowgirl boots, which are much shorter, and ornamented with fancy stitching.
1927   Chillicothe (Missouri) Daily Tribune 4 Aug. 1/4   Three young women representing the XIX Club appeared in the business district resplendent in cowgirl boots, tomboy skirts and Mexican sombreros and carrying rope lariats.
1949   Rappahannock Rec. (Kilmarnock, Va.) 22 Dec. 21/2   Dear Santa: please come to see me as this is my second Xmas and bring me a baby doll, cooking set, cowgirl boots, red sweater,..and anything else you think will suit me.
2020   Los Angeles Times 24 Aug. a1   A girl in a lacy maroon dress, pink cowgirl boots and a pink cloth mask giggled as she chased her friends.

1925—2020(Hide quotations)

 
Thesaurus »
Categories »
 

  cowgirl hat   n. a broad-brimmed hat with a high soft crown, of a style associated with cowgirls; cf. cowboy boot n. at cowboy n. Compounds 4.

1897   Lamar (Colorado) Reg. 25 Sept.   Cowgirl hats at Irwin's.
1917   Cosmopolitan July 144/2   I take that cowgirl hat from its retreat and read its inscription ‘Give 'er pep and let 'er buck’.
2018   Paisley Daily Express (Nexis) 25 Apr. 3   Put your cowboy or cowgirl hats on and get involved in a rootin', tootin' hoedown.

1897—2018(Hide quotations)
