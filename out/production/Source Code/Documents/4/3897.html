Forms:  see anti- prefix   and ageing adj.(Show Less)
Origin: Fromed within English, by derivation. Etymons: anti- prefix, ageing adj.
Etymology: < anti- prefix + ageing adj.(Show Less)
 

  Designed to counter or slow the process of ageing; esp. (of a product or technique) claiming to reduce or reverse the effects of ageing on a person's appearance.

1917   U.S. Patent 1,249,180 2/2   A process for treating rubber or similar material which comprises subjecting the rubber to a vulcanizing agent containing oxygen in the presence of anti-aging material, and inducing vulcanization to take place by the action thereof.
1946   Nature 24 Aug. 277/1   The combination of androgenic, oestrogenic and thyroid hormones together produces an apparent ‘anti-ageing’ effect.
2011   Esquire July 61/1   This is an anti-ageing moisturiser with a wealth of botanical ingredients.

1917—2011(Hide quotations)
