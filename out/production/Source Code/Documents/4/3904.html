Frequency (in current use):  Show frequency band information
Origin: Formed within English, by derivation. Etymons: anti- prefix, sweat n.
Etymology: < anti- prefix + sweat n.(Show Less)
 

  Intended to limit sweating or protect against its effects; esp. designating an insulating rug or sheet designed to prevent a sweating horse from becoming chilled.

1886   Hosp. Formulary (Dept. Public Charities & Correction, N.Y.) (ed. 3) 82   Pilulæ contra Sudorem... ‘Anti-Sweat Pill’.
1905   Farm Jrnl. Dec. 417/3 (advt.)    Hameless Horse Collars Anti-sweat Pad.
1963   E. H. Edwards Saddlery xxi. 158   Also known in the stable as well as for travelling and in the paddock is the now familiar anti-sweat rug known in its original form as the Aerborn sheet.
1991   J. Cooper Polo 257   Both their lorries were green, and so were their lead reins, anti-sweat sheets, buckets and bandages.
2009   N.Y. Times (Nexis) 23 July d3   It's easy to ignore pillows marketed for their ‘anti-sweat’ properties, unless you (or a mate) have night sweats.

1886—2009(Hide quotations)
