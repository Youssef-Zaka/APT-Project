Holy Hour, n.

Pronunciation:

, 
U.S. Hear pronunciation/ˈhoʊli ˌaʊ(ə)r/
, 
Irish English /ˈhoʊlɪ ˌaʊ(ə)r/
Forms:  also with lower-case initials.(Show Less)
Origin: Formed within English, by compounding. Etymons: holy adj., hour n.
Etymology: < holy adj. + hour n.
In sense 1   after French heure sainte (1731 or earlier); the practice was introduced by Marguerite-Marie Alacoque in Paray-le-Monial, France.
 
In sense 2   with allusion to the religious motivation behind this requirement.
(Show Less)
Categories »
 

 1. In the Roman Catholic Church: a devotional exercise or religious observance consisting of an hour of prayer or meditation in the presence of the consecrated Eucharist.

1869   Month Dec. 629   The beautiful Devotion of the Holy Hour, by which the Faithful are invited to spend that hour in the week which corresponds to our Blessed Lord's Agony in the Garden in prayer and meditation.
1896   Donahoe's Mag. Jan. 94/1   I called on a friend..and she invited me to church for the ‘Holy Hour’.
1914   Irish Examiner 6 Mar. 4   The Devotion of the Holy Hour will take place at SS. Peter and Paul's Church from 2.30 to 3.30 today.
2016   Bangor (Maine) Daily News (Nexis) 31 Mar.   The Holy Hour will include Eucharistic adoration, music, silence, and the Chaplet of Divine Mercy.

1869—2016(Hide quotations)

 
Thesaurus »
Categories »
 

 2. Irish English colloquial. A period of time in the afternoon during which pubs are required to close. Now historical.This requirement was introduced in some parts of Ireland in the 1920s and was discontinued at the end of the 20th cent.

1953   H. Tracy Mind You, I've said Nothing vii. 89   The pub was just closing for the Holy Hour as I came up to it and entry could only be gained by a subterfuge.
1977   M. Begley Rambles in Ireland (1978) 193   Dublin & Cork: Pubs close for ‘Holy Hour’ 2:30-3:30 p.m.
2019   Irish Times (Nexis) 11 May (Opinion section) 15   The famous ‘Holy Hour’..wasn't always as successful as temperance campaigners wished, often locking customers in rather than out.

1953—2019(Hide quotations)
