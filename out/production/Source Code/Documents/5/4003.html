  The action of engaging in plámás (plámás n.); flattery; insincere or exaggerated praise, especially as a means to cajole or persuade.

1897   Kerry (Ireland) Sentinel 23 Oct.   John O'Donnell..has managed by the strength of his..plotting and plamausing to make Lower Bridge Street the top of the Town.
1998   T. P. Dolan Dict. Hiberno-Eng. 200/1   Stop your plámásing and tell me what you really think about my new coat.
2019   Irish Times (Nexis) 26 Apr. 5   He could..bend you to his will with a combination of plámásing and gregariousness.
