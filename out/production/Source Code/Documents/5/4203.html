U.S. Hear pronunciation/ˈnɛðərwərd/
Forms:  see nether adv.1   and -ward suffix; also early Middle English noþerwad (transmission error), 1500s–1600s 1800s– netherward. (Show Less)
Frequency (in current use):  Show frequency band information
Origin: Formed within English, by derivation. Etymons: nether adv.1, -ward suffix.
Etymology: < nether adv.1 + -ward suffix. Compare netheward adj., netherwards adv.(Show Less)
 A. adv.
Thesaurus »
Categories »
 

  Downwards; to the bottom. Now rare.

OE   Ælfric Gram. (St. John's Oxf.) 238   Deorsum uersum, nyðerwerd.
OE   Aldhelm Glosses (Digby 146) in A. S. Napier Old Eng. Glosses (1900) 105/2   In præceps : nyþerwyrd.
c1175  (▸OE)    Ælfric Homily (Bodl. 343) in S. Irvine Old Eng. Homilies (1993) 41   He tealde niðerweard..& Lucas tealde upweard.
c1275  (▸?c1250)    Owl & Nightingale (Calig.) (1935) 144 (MED)   Þos hule luste þiderward & hold hire eȝe noþerwad [read noþerward; a1300 Jesus Oxf. neþerward].
a1300  (▸c1275)    Physiologus (1991) 394   Ðe mereman is a meiden ilike: On brest & on bodi oc..Fro ðe noule niðerward ne is ȝe no man like.
c1540  (▸?a1400)    Gest Historiale Destr. Troy 7717   Fro the Nauell netherward he was an able horse, And euyn made as a man fro the medill vp.
a1640   T. Risdon Chorogr. Surv. Devon (1811) (modernized text) §215 224   Netherward to Great Lynd.
1845   Littell's Living Age 22 Feb. 497/1   The poet, or artist generally, is a conductor of the power of imagination, open to receive it from above—open to transmit it netherward.
1894   R. Burton in Atlantic Monthly Apr. 482/1   Under the misty nesses netherward falls, The flood 'neath the earth.
1950   M. Peake Gormenghast xxii. 146   Barquentine spat on the heavy stone..and sent it netherward on its resounding journey.

OE—1950(Hide quotations)

 
 B. adj.
 

†1. Turned downwards. Obsolete.

OE   Riddle 21 1   Neb is min niþerweard.

OE—OE(Hide quotations)

 
Thesaurus »
Categories »
 

 2. Lower, low-lying, bottommost. Now rare.

a1300  (▸?OE)    Bounds (Sawyer 244) in E. H. Bates Two Cartularies of Benedictine Abbeys of Muchelney & Athelney (1899) 96   In oriente the Rede leygh et deinde on Lanschareweye nytherwardon.
 
1865–6   W. Whitman When Lilacs last in Door-yard Bloom'd 6   I watch'd where you pass'd and was lost in the netherward black of the night.
1878   T. Hardy Return of Native I. i. vii. 149   But celestial imperiousness, love, wrath, and fervour had proved to be somewhat thrown away on netherward Egdon.
1887   Littell's Living Age 9 July 87/2   One prescription desires that ‘the bowels of an earwig be pounded with the smede of wheaten meal and the netherward part (i.e., root) of marche, and mingled with honey’.

a1300—1887(Hide quotations)
