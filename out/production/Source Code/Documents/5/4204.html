, 
U.S. Hear pronunciation/pædʒ/
Frequency (in current use):  Show frequency band information
Origin: Probably from a proper name. Etymon: proper name Padge.
Etymology: Probably < Padge, rhyming form of Madge   (see madge n.1). Compare pudge n.3(Show Less)
English regional (midlands). Now rare.

  The barn owl, Tyto alba. Also padge-owl.

1848   A. B. Evans Leicestershire Words 65   Padge-owl, the common owl.
1881   S. Evans Evans's Leicestershire Words (new ed.) 208   Padge,..the common barn-owl.
1885   C. Swainson Provinc. Names Brit. Birds 126   Familiar names [of the barn owl]... Padge, Pudge, or Pudge owl (Leicestershire).
a1903   J. H. Brown in Eng. Dial. Dict. (1903) IV. 406/2   [Nottinghamshire] Padge [the barn owl, Strix flammea].
