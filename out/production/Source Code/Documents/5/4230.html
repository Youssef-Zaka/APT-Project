† wasteheart, int.

Forms: 

α. 1600s wee'st heart, 1700s wa-ist heart, 1700s wae'st heart, 1700s–1800s waste heart, 1800s waast-heart, 1800s waasteheart, 1800s waestart, 1800s wast-heart, 1800s wasteheart, 1800s–1900s wae's t'heart.

Frequency (in current use):  Show frequency band information
Origin: Formed within English, by conversion. Etymon: English wae is (the) heart.
Etymology: < wae is (the) heart; compare similar expressions at woe adj. 1a.(Show Less)
English regional (chiefly Yorkshire) and Scottish. Obsolete.
Thesaurus »
Categories »
 

  Used to express grief, pity, regret, disappointment, or concern: ‘alas!’ ‘woe is me!’ Also wasteheart-a-day, wasteheart of me.

1695   W. Congreve Love for Love ii. i. 18   Fore. Nurse, Where's your young Mistress? Nurse. Wee'st heart, I know not, they're none of 'em come home yet.
1703   R. Thoresby Let. 27 Apr. in J. Ray Corr. (1848) 430   Wae'st heart, a condolence to the same purport with wae's me, woe is the heart, &c.
a1774   R. Fergusson Poems (1785) 141   'Mang men, wae's-heart! we aften find The brawest drest want peace o' mind.
1787   F. Grose Provinc. Gloss.   Wa-ist heart, woe is me. N.
1827   C. I. Johnstone Elizabeth De Bruce III. ii. 25   Waes heart! waes heart!
1829   J. Hunter Hallamshire Gloss.   Wast-heart-a-day, an expression of grief or of commiseration.
1835   E. Strutt Chances & Changes II. xvii. 308   Wae's-heart—it's a vexatious world we have to go through.
1854   Notes & Queries 1st Ser. 9 349/2   Waestart, a common expression of sorrow or condolence among the lower classes..around Leeds.
1862   C. C. Robinson Dial. Leeds & Neighbourhood 445   ‘Wāasteheart barns! ye knawn't what's let on yuh, God help yuh!’ ‘How are yuh to-dāay then like?’ ‘Noa better, thenk yuh.’ ‘Ah, wāasteheart! wāasteheart!’
1876   F. K. Robinson Gloss. Words Whitby   Weeas is t'heart!, I am heartily sorry for it.
a1912   W. W. Skeat & A. L. Mayhew Gloss. Tudor & Stuart Words (1914) 445   ‘Wae's t'heart’, ‘Wae's heart of me’, are Yorks. exclamations.
