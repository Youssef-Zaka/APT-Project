  A person who naps or habitually takes a nap.

a1450   MS Lansdowne 763 f. 60v   Janglers cum japers, nappers, galpers, quoque drawers, Momlers [etc.]
c1450  (▸?a1400)    Duke Rowland & Sir Otuell (1880) 288   Cheualrye es fro hym gone, A nolde nappere als he were.
 
1730   Fog's Weekly Jrnl. 14 Nov. 1/1   These are all very moderate Nappers, compar'd to the famous Seven Sleepers.
1823   I. Pocock Nigel iv. iii. 76   Strap. What, it's ill manners for a man To peep at's own wife napping! Nigel. But that napper Is not your wife.
1866   Harper's Weekly 10 Feb. 91/3   The old proverb, ‘He who would thrive must rise at five’, is constantly sounded in the ears of sleepy morning nappers.
a1943   L. Hart Wandering Minstrel in Compl. Lyrics (1986) 45/2   The weariest of nappers awakens to his notes, And fourteen-year-old flappers tell Gaelic anecdotes.
1987   K. Morgan Sleep & Ageing ii. 41   The remaining nine were not habitual nappers.
